üîç Hidden Dynamics Where the real problems live

These systemic issues don't appear in official statistics but determine whether interventions succeed or fail. Investment here creates leverage.

The Final-Use Blind Spot

  • Invisible waste: Only 15-20% of primary energy delivers useful services ‚Äî 80%+ lost before reaching end-use
  • Supply obsession: Policy measures capacity addition (GW), not services delivered (lumens, p-km, ton-hours)
  • Measurement gap: No tracking of useful energy ‚Äî we measure kWh sold, not outcomes achieved
  • Investment opportunity: Energy services framing, outcome-based metrics, exergy accounting

The Pump Subsidy Trap

  • Perverse incentives: Free agricultural power ‚Üí <2% of 30M pumps exceed 40% efficiency [BEE]
  • Political capture: Electricity subsidies are electoral currency ‚Äî 25-40% savings potential untapped
  • Hidden costs: 33 TWh/year wasted (‚Çπ20K+ Cr); efficiency can't compete with "free"
  • Investment opportunity: Income support decoupled from consumption, feeder-level metering, ESCO models

The Labeling Theater

  • AC testing fraud: Lab test at 35¬∞C; real summer at 45-50¬∞C ‚Üí 5-star performs worse than 1-star [CSE]
  • Performance gap: ACs consume 17-28% more power than rated at real temperatures
  • Standards lag: India tests for mild climate; ignores ISO hot climate protocol (46¬∞C outside)
  • Investment opportunity: Real-world testing protocols, dynamic efficiency ratings, enforcement

The Cooking Energy Tragedy

  • Massive waste: 700M people use chulhas at 8-10% efficiency ‚Äî 90% of fuel wasted as heat/smoke [CWE]
  • Health toll: 482,000 deaths/year from household air pollution (2017)
  • LPG gap: PMUY distributed connections but refill rates low due to cost
  • Investment opportunity: Improved biomass stoves, electric cooking, biogas systems

The Generation Bias

  • Policy asymmetry: RE gets viability gap funding + RPOs; efficiency gets voluntary programs
  • Lobbying imbalance: Solar/wind industry organized (SECI, MNRE); efficiency diffuse, no constituency
  • Grid planning blind: CEA projects capacity addition; never models demand reduction scenarios
  • Investment opportunity: Negawatt markets, efficiency certificates, demand response as capacity

The Thermal Power Lock-in

  • Stranded efficiency: Coal plants at 35% efficiency (design 38%) ‚Äî 65% of fuel wasted [Prayas]
  • Age penalty: Efficiency degrades 1-2% for plants >25 years ‚Äî but kept running for political reasons
  • Merit order ignored: Efficient plants underutilized while inefficient plants run for state revenue
  • Investment opportunity: Efficiency-based dispatch, accelerated retirement, waste heat recovery
