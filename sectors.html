The Data Integrity Problem

  • Perverse incentives: Contractors paid by tonnage overreport; ULBs underreport failures
  • Measurement blind spots: Data captured at dumpsites misses informal diversion entirely
  • Self-reported metrics: "97% collection" claims unverified ‚Äî no GPS/IoT validation
  • Investment opportunity: Real-time waste tracking infrastructure, independent audits

The Informal Sector Paradox

  • Hidden truth: Informal workers achieve 80%+ of actual recycling in India
  • Formalization backfires: Large company contracts often exclude pickers, recycling rates drop
  • Value extraction: Aggregators capture 90% of material value; pickers get 5-10%
  • Investment opportunity: Picker-owned cooperatives, direct market access platforms

The Tipping Fee Trap

  • Guaranteed minimums: PPP contracts guarantee X tonnes/day ‚Äî incentivizes against reduction
  • Technology lock-in: ‚Çπ500+ Cr WtE plants need feedstock forever ‚Äî lobby against composting
  • Hidden subsidies: WtE gets grid purchase agreements; composting/recycling get nothing
  • Investment opportunity: Outcome-based contracts, recycling-first infrastructure

The Segregation Failure Chain

  • No consequences: Mixed waste still collected; household penalties never enforced
  • Worker convenience: Staff remix segregated waste ‚Äî different streams go to same place anyway
  • Infrastructure mismatch: Single-bin trucks, no dual-stream MRFs negate household effort
  • Investment opportunity: End-to-end segregated infrastructure, enforcement tech

The Hazardous Leakage

  • Healthcare mixing: Biomedical waste from small clinics enters MSW stream
  • Informal e-waste processing: Acid baths, open burning in residential areas
  • No accessible drop-offs: Batteries, pharma, paint end up in household waste
  • Investment opportunity: Distributed hazardous collection, clinic compliance systems

The Legacy Waste Political Economy

  • Land mafia nexus: Dumpsites on valuable land; remediation unlocks real estate deals
  • Biomining theater: Some "remediation" just moves waste elsewhere
  • Externalities ignored: Healthcare costs of surrounding communities never counted
  • Investment opportunity: Independent remediation verification, health impact bonds
